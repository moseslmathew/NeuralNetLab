Why this matters for AI: Training a neural network = multiplying millions of matrix values — simple, repetitive math done billions of times. A CPU handles 4 tasks at once (great for complex logic). A GPU handles 100 tasks simultaneously (perfect for uniform parallel computation).

GPT-3 training — what we actually know
GPT-3 has 175 billion parameters and required enormous compute to train. OpenAI never officially disclosed exact hardware details — but based on the 2020 timeline, training likely ran on thousands of V100 GPUs (not A100s, which were barely released then), using both model parallelism (splitting layers across GPUs) and data parallelism (each GPU processes different data batches). Training duration estimates of ~30 days exist but are unconfirmed by OpenAI.
About this simulation: The 100-core grid here represents a tiny fraction of what one real GPU does in one cycle — real training involves thousands of CUDA cores, dedicated Tensor cores, high-bandwidth memory, and fast GPU interconnects working together simultaneously.
